## Promotion Process

### How Promotions Work

**Step 1: Self-Assessment (Continuous)**
- Track your accomplishments
- Compare against next level criteria
- Identify gaps
- Discuss with manager in 1:1s

**Step 2: Manager Nomination (Quarterly)**
- Manager identifies promotion candidates
- Submits promotion packet with evidence
- Includes peer feedback and accomplishments

**Step 3: Calibration (Quarterly)**
- Leadership reviews all nominations
- Ensures consistent standards
- Discusses edge cases

**Step 4: Assessment (If needed)**
- Technical assessment for IC promotions
- Leadership scenarios for manager promotions
- Panel interview with senior leaders

**Step 5: Decision & Communication (Quarterly)**
- CEO approves final promotions
- Effective next quarter
- Public celebration in all-hands

### Promotion Cycles

**Q1 (January)** - Promotions effective April 1  
**Q2 (April)** - Promotions effective July 1  
**Q3 (July)** - Promotions effective October 1  
**Q4 (October)** - Promotions effective January 1

**Off-cycle promotions** - Rare, CEO approval required

### What Happens When Promoted

**Within 1 week:**
- âœ… Title updated in all systems
- âœ… Compensation adjustment (see below)
- âœ… New responsibilities documented
- âœ… Announcement in all-hands

**Within 1 month:**

## Demotion

### When Demotion Occurs

Demotion happens when:
- Performance consistently below level expectations
- Unable to fulfill role responsibilities
- Better fit at lower level
- Organizational restructuring

**Demotion is not punishment** - it's realignment.

### Demotion Process

**Step 1: Performance Discussion (Week 0)**
- Manager discusses performance concerns
- Creates improvement plan
- Documents expectations

**Step 2: Improvement Period (90 days)**
- Weekly 1:1s with specific feedback
- Measurable goals and milestones
- Support and coaching provided
- Progress documented

**Step 3: Evaluation (Day 90)**
- Review against improvement plan
- Decide: continue, promote, or demote

**Step 4: Demotion Decision**
- Manager recommendation
- HR + Leadership review
- CEO approval (for senior roles)

**Step 5: Communication (if demoting)**
- Private 1:1 meeting
- Explain rationale clearly
- Document new role and compensation
- Create path forward

### Compensation Adjustments

**Immediate (Effective with demotion):**
- Salary moves to top of lower band
- Benefits remain unchanged
- PTO/vacation unchanged

**Example:**
```
Senior Developer (450,000 LKR/month) â†’ Developer
New salary: 250,000 LKR/month (top of IC2 band)
Reduction: 200,000 LKR/month (44%)
```

**Severance Alternative:**
- If reduction > 40%, offer 3-month severance + termination
- Employee chooses: accept demotion or take severance

### Path Back to Promotion

**Returning to higher level requires:**
1. âœ… Minimum 12 months at lower level
2. âœ… "Exceeds Expectations" performance
3. âœ… Demonstrated capability at higher level
4. âœ… Standard promotion process
